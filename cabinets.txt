SHAKER CABINET DOORS WITH BALTIC BIRCH PLYWOOD PANELS
======================================================

FRAME (RAIL & STILE) WOOD
-------------------------
Use SOLID HARDWOOD for the frames (stiles and rails), NOT plywood.

- Staining / clear finish: use SOLID BIRCH to match the Baltic birch panel.
- Painting: use a stable, tight-grained hardwood:
    * Soft maple - the go-to for paint-grade work; cheap, stable, smooth.
    * Poplar - cheaper, paints fine, but softer and dents more easily.
    * Hard maple - excellent, slight overkill.
  Avoid open-grained woods (oak, ash, hickory) for paint - the grain
  telegraphs through.

- Do NOT use plywood for the frame itself - exposed edge plies and
  joinery won't hold or look right.
- Let the panel "float" in the groove (don't glue) is standard, though
  plywood is stable enough that gluing it in for rigidity is also fine.


FRAME THICKNESS
---------------
- Standard: 3/4" (about 19mm) thick stock - matches S4S hardwood and
  is the default for cabinet door work.
- Bump to 7/8" only for inset doors or unusually large doors needing
  extra rigidity.


FRAME WIDTH (the visible face)
------------------------------
- 2" to 2-1/4" - classic Shaker proportion, most common.
- Up to 2-1/2" for larger doors to stay visually balanced.
- Bottom rail sometimes wider (2-1/2" to 3") on tall doors for a
  traditional look; equal-width members give a cleaner modern look.


PANEL GROOVE WIDTH
------------------
Baltic birch is METRIC - "1/4" sheet is actually 6mm (~0.236"),
about 1/64" under a true 1/4". A standard 1/4" groove leaves the
panel loose and rattling.

- ALWAYS caliper the actual sheet before cutting (thickness varies
  between batches/suppliers).
- Target a snug slip fit - panel slides in with light hand pressure.

How to get the right width:
  1. Dedicated undersized "plywood dado" bit/blade (6mm / 0.236" slot).
  2. Two passes on the table saw, nudging the fence to dial in the fit.
  3. Undersized dado/router - start at 7/32" and creep up.

Quick reference:
  Panel actual thickness   ->   Target groove width
  6 mm (~0.236")                ~0.236"-0.240" (snug slip fit)
  True 1/4" (0.250")            0.250"-0.255"

Note: cope-and-stick bit sets cut a fixed ~1/4" groove, which is why
6mm panels rattle in them - shim the panel or use a plywood-sized set.


PANEL GROOVE DEPTH
------------------
- Standard: 3/8" deep on 3/4" stock.
    * On cope-and-stick, the groove depth = the stub tenon/cope depth,
      giving a strong joint and good glue surface.
    * Leaves plenty of material on a 2"+ stile.
- 1/4" acceptable on smaller doors or thinner stock (less strength).
- Deeper than 3/8" is unnecessary.


PANEL SIZING
------------
- Cut panel to the groove-to-groove dimension MINUS ~1/16" total
  (~1/32" per side) so it doesn't bottom out hard.
- This lets the frame joints close up tight rather than the panel
  holding them apart. Plywood moves little, but the slight gap is
  still good practice.


SUMMARY
-------
- Frame: solid birch (stain) or soft maple/poplar (paint)
- Frame stock: 3/4" thick, 2"-2-1/4" wide
- Groove width: matched to actual 6mm panel (~0.236", snug)
- Groove depth: 3/8"
- Panel: cut ~1/16" undersize, float or glue (plywood is stable)
